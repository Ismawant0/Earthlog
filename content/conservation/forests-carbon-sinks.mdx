Climate change is one of the greatest challenges of our era. Carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) levels in the atmosphere have reached record highs, trapping heat and warming the globe. While transitioning to clean energy is essential, we must also remove existing carbon from the air. This is where carbon sinks come in.

A carbon sink is anything that absorbs more carbon from the atmosphere than it releases. The earth’s oceans, soils, and forests are massive, natural carbon sinks.

### The Chemistry of a Tree
Through photosynthesis, trees pull $CO_2$ from the air, combine it with water, and use sunlight to synthesize sugars that fuel their growth. The carbon is literally woven into the wood, bark, roots, and soil, while oxygen is released back into the atmosphere:

$$\text{Carbon Dioxide} + \text{Water} + \text{Sunlight} \rightarrow \text{Glucose} + \text{Oxygen}$$

Over its lifetime, a single mature tree can absorb more than 22 kilograms of $CO_2$ per year. When multiplied by millions of trees, the impact is substantial.

### Planting vs. Protecting
While planting new trees is crucial, protecting existing mature forests is equally vital. Mature trees have vast root networks and high biomass, storing far more carbon than young saplings. Destabilizing or clearing these forests releases centuries of stored carbon back into the atmosphere instantly.

Therefore, our movement focuses on two complementary fronts:
- **Nurturing New Growth:** Planting native tree species in deforested areas or urban settings.
- **Guardianship:** Caring for local woodlands, preventing illegal logging, and preserving soil health.

Every tree planted and cared for is a living carbon filter that will protect the climate for generations.
