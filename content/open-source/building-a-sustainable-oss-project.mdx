Open Source Software (OSS) drives the modern internet. However, building a project that remains active, healthy, and funded over the long term requires intentional planning and structures.

## 1. Choosing the Right License

Your choice of license dictates how others can use, modify, and distribute your code:

- **Permissive (MIT, Apache 2.0):** Maximum flexibility. Users can include your code in proprietary projects.
- **Copyleft (GPL v3):** Protects community modifications. Anyone modifying the code must also share their changes.

## 2. Governance Models

A clear governance model determines how decisions are made:

- **Benevolent Dictator for Life (BDFL):** Single founder makes final decisions.
- **Steering Committee:** Group of core maintainers votes on features and direction.
- **Foundation Support:** Transitioning projects to organisations like CNCF or Linux Foundation.
